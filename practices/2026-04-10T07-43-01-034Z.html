You have a Microsoft Dataverse environment.

You create a business process flow for a table. The business process flow has three stages.

You also create a business rule that must run whenever the value of a status column changes. The business rule must run after each stage of the business process flow is complete.

You need to ensure the business rule is active and will run as designed.

What should you do?

Select only one answer.

The correct approach is to activate the business rule on the table, because business rules are always defined and enforced at the table level, not inside a business process flow. Activating the rule on the table ensures it runs automatically when the status column value changes, including after each stage of the BPF is completed. Activating a business rule in the business process flow itself is not possible, and adding it as a step in the flow would not trigger the rule properly. Activating the BPF on the table enables the process flow but does not affect whether the business rule executes.
